## Delivery Estimates and Limitations

All delivery timelines provided at checkout are estimates based on standard logistics performance metrics and carrier service level commitments. These timelines are not guaranteed and are subject to variation based on factors including but not limited to weather disruptions, customs clearance delays, carrier backlogs, regional infrastructure limitations, public holidays, and high-volume periods such as major retail events.

Customers are advised to consider these variables when placing time-sensitive orders. The platform does not accept liability for business or consequential losses arising from delayed deliveries where the delay is within the range of normal variability for the selected shipping method.

Estimated delivery windows are calculated at the time of order placement using the best available carrier data. Changes in carrier routing, consolidation, or handoff may alter these estimates after order confirmation. Customers can monitor real-time tracking data through the platform's order tracking interface or the carrier's tracking portal.

## Delayed Delivery Handling

Delays in delivery do not automatically qualify for compensation, refund, or order cancellation. However, exceptions and escalation paths may apply under the following conditions:

- Delivery exceeds 10 calendar days beyond the upper bound of the estimated delivery window for standard shipping
- Delay exceeds 5 calendar days for express or guaranteed delivery services where a specific date was communicated
- Delay directly results in product degradation, making the item unusable (particularly relevant for perishable goods — see Perishable Goods policy)
- The delay is caused by a documented carrier error attributable to the platform's contracted logistics arrangement

If the product remains usable and functional despite the delay, refund eligibility may be denied. Customers who wish to return a delayed but usable item should follow the standard return process within the applicable return window.

### Compensation for Guaranteed Delivery Failures

If the customer purchased a shipping guarantee or expedited service that promised delivery by a specific date, and that guarantee is not met, the customer is entitled to:

- A full refund of the additional shipping fee paid for the expedited service
- In cases of significant disruption, store credit may be offered at agent discretion

This compensation applies only to the shipping fee component and not to the product value, unless the product itself is impacted (e.g., perishables that degraded).

## Lost Package Determination

A package is classified as lost under the following conditions:

- No tracking updates for more than 7 consecutive calendar days during active transit (not counting weekends for some carriers)
- The carrier formally confirms loss after completing their internal investigation process
- Delivery status remains stuck in a pre-delivery state beyond a reasonable timeframe defined by the carrier's service level agreement
- The last known tracking scan shows a location inconsistent with the delivery route at a time that is disproportionately long ago

### Process for reporting a potentially lost package:

1. Customer contacts platform support after the 7-day tracking silence threshold
2. Agent initiates carrier investigation through the logistics portal
3. Carrier investigation is conducted over a period of 5–7 business days in most cases
4. If carrier confirms the loss, or if no resolution is reached within 14 calendar days, the package is classified as lost

### Entitlements upon confirmed loss:

Once a package is formally classified as lost, the customer is entitled to choose one of the following:

- Full refund to the original payment method, processed within 3–5 business days of loss confirmation
- Replacement shipment of the same item (subject to stock availability and additional shipping timeline)

If the customer chooses a replacement and the item is out of stock, a full refund is automatically processed.

## Delivered but Not Received Claims

In cases where the carrier's tracking system indicates successful delivery (including a delivery confirmation timestamp and potentially a delivery photograph or signature) but the customer claims they did not receive the package, the following investigation process applies:

1. Customer must verify with household members, neighbors, building management, or local parcel collection points
2. Customer provides a statement confirming non-receipt, along with any relevant additional context (e.g., secure building access, travel during delivery window)
3. The platform initiates a carrier investigation, which includes review of delivery photographs, GPS confirmation data, signature records (if applicable), and driver route logs
4. If the carrier confirms delivery with sufficient evidence, the refund request may be denied
5. If the carrier's evidence is inconclusive or contradictory, the case is escalated for manual review

For high-value orders (typically above $250), additional verification steps may apply, including confirming with the carrier whether an adult signature was required and whether that requirement was met.

If the claim cannot be substantiated and carrier evidence supports successful delivery, the refund request may be denied. However, agents retain discretion to offer goodwill resolutions for first-time non-receipt claims from customers with a strong account history.

## Multi-Package Orders and Partial Delivery

Orders dispatched in multiple shipments may result in partial delivery scenarios:

- If one shipment is delivered and another is missing, each shipment is evaluated independently
- The customer should not wait for all shipments before reporting a missing package from a multi-package order
- Refunds or replacements are calculated proportionally for the undelivered portion

## Return-to-Sender Situations

If a package is returned to the sender due to:

- Incorrect or incomplete delivery address provided by the customer
- Failed delivery attempts where the customer was unavailable and did not collect from the depot
- Customer refused delivery without a valid reason

The customer may be offered a reshipment at their cost (new shipping fee) or a refund minus applicable handling and return shipping charges. Refunds for return-to-sender situations are typically processed within 7–10 business days after the package arrives back at the fulfillment center.

## Fraud Prevention Measures

Repeated claims of lost or undelivered packages may trigger automated and manual fraud detection mechanisms. Accounts exhibiting suspicious patterns are subject to:

- Temporary restriction of loss/non-delivery claim eligibility
- Enhanced verification requirements for future claims (e.g., mandatory carrier investigation before refund approval)
- Manual review of claim history before any further action
- In severe cases, denial of compensation requests and account status review

Customers who believe their account has been incorrectly flagged may appeal through the standard escalation process with supporting documentation.

---

## International Shipping Considerations

For orders shipped internationally, additional considerations apply:

- Customs clearance delays of up to 15 business days are within the normal range and do not trigger the delayed delivery compensation policy
- Customs duties, import taxes, and clearance fees are the responsibility of the customer unless explicitly stated otherwise at checkout
- International lost package investigations may take longer (up to 21 business days) due to cross-border carrier coordination requirements
- Packages seized by customs authorities are not treated as lost packages; resolution depends on the nature of the customs hold

---

## Escalation Scenarios

Escalation is required when:

- Carrier and customer reports conflict materially and cannot be reconciled at agent level
- High-value shipments (above $500) are involved in any loss or non-delivery claim
- Evidence is insufficient to determine outcome within standard guidelines
- Customer disputes the carrier investigation outcome
- Multiple lost package claims have been made by the same customer within a rolling 90-day window
- International carrier investigation exceeds 21 business days without resolution

### Appendix A: Detailed Application Guidelines and Training Scenarios In order to ensure that all agents apply this policy correctly across diverse customer scenarios, the following detailed guidelines and case studies have been appended to this document. These scenarios are drawn from historical support logs and represent common edge cases where policy application requires nuanced judgment. It is imperative that all support staff review these scenarios regularly. The consistent application of policy is the foundation of our customer trust and regulatory compliance program. 1. **Scenario Assessment Methodology:** Before reviewing historical cases, agents must understand the core methodology for scenario assessment. First, immediately establish the factual timeline of the customer interaction. This means reviewing all system timestamps, starting from the exact moment of order placement, up to the precise second the carrier logged a delivery event. Do not rely on customer accounts of timeframes if they conflict with system logs. Second, review the customer’s interaction history. Are there past instances where similar policies were applied? If so, continuity in decision-making is preferred, unless the previous decision was explicitly marked as an error in the quality assurance (QA) audit. Third, consult the inventory management system to confirm the exact SKU and its assigned categorization at the time the order was placed. Categories can occasionally shift due to catalog updates; the category active at the time of purchase is the governing one. 2. **Cross-Departmental Coordination Protocol:** Often, policy resolution requires input from departments outside standard tier-1 support. For example, involving the logistics liaison team is necessary when carrier tracking states a package was delivered, but GPS coordinates suggest misdelivery to a neighboring postal code. In such cases, agents must open a sub-ticket tagged for the logistics team and place the main customer interaction in a pending state for no more than 48 hours. When coordinating with the finance department regarding complex refund routing (e.g., split payments involving store credit and an expired credit card), agents must provide the exact transaction ledger IDs. Do not attempt to manually calculate prorated refunds for multi-item orders with partial cancellations; always use the automated refund calculation tool. 3. **Regulatory Audit Readiness:** It is critical to recognize that every ticket resolved under this policy may be subject to external regulatory audit, particularly in jurisdictions with robust consumer protection authorities. Therefore, the case notes must be written in clear, professional language that explicitly cites the relevant section of this policy document. For example, rather than writing "Denied return because item is opened," agents must write "Return request denied pursuant to Section 3 of the Hygiene-Restricted Products policy, as photographic evidence confirmed the factory seal was broken." This level of precision protects the platform during compliance reviews. 4. **Handling Customer Escalation Threats:** Customers may occasionally threaten to involve third-party dispute resolution services, credit card chargebacks, or social media exposure when a policy is enforced against their favor. Agents must remain objective and de-escalate without compromising the integrity of the policy. The standard response should calmly reiterate the policy provision, explain the rationale behind it, and outline the formal internal appeals process if one applies. Agents are authorized to offer a one-time goodwill credit of up to $15 to de-escalate tense situations, provided the customer account is in good standing and the interaction does not involve suspected fraud. This goodwill credit must not be framed as an admission of fault or a reversal of the policy decision. 5. **System Downtime Contingencies:** In the event that the primary order management system experiences degraded performance or a full outage, agents must not issue definitive resolutions based on incomplete cached data. Instead, invoke the standard downtime communication protocol, informing the customer that their request has been logged and will be processed immediately upon system restoration. If a policy deadline (e.g., the end of a 30-day return window) expires during a system outage, the system will automatically grant a grace period equivalent to the duration of the outage plus 24 hours. Agents manually reviewing such cases must apply this grace period proactively. 6. **Continuous Improvement Feedback Loop:** The contents of this policy are subject to quarterly review by the Global Policy Committee. Agents who identify recurring customer friction points or ambiguities in the text are encouraged to submit annotated examples through the internal policy feedback portal.
