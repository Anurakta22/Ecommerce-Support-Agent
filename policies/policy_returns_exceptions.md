## Overview of Exceptions and Policy Hierarchy

While the general return policy allows customers to return eligible items within a specified period, certain categories are subject to stricter controls due to safety, regulatory, or logistical constraints. These exception rules override the standard return policy wherever applicable. In cases where a product falls under multiple policy categories, the most restrictive rule shall take precedence, unless explicitly stated otherwise.

Exception handling is designed to balance customer satisfaction with operational feasibility, ensuring that unsafe, non-resalable, or legally restricted goods are not reintroduced into inventory. This framework is reviewed periodically to align with changes in consumer law, carrier regulations, and product safety standards.

Supporting agents should familiarize themselves with the full hierarchy before resolving any case involving a restricted category. Misjudging category classification or applying the wrong rule set can result in invalid decisions, increased escalation rates, and potential legal exposure. When in doubt, escalate rather than guess.

## Final Sale Items and Limitations

Items designated as "Final Sale" are explicitly excluded from return, refund, or exchange eligibility under normal circumstances. These items are typically discounted, clearance inventory, or limited-edition products where resale logistics are constrained. The Final Sale designation is applied at the product level and is visible to customers at the point of purchase, including on the product detail page, in the cart, and on the order confirmation.

Customers who purchase Final Sale items acknowledge and agree to the non-returnable designation as a condition of purchase. This acknowledgment is captured at checkout and may be referenced in dispute resolution.

However, exceptions may be granted under narrowly defined conditions:

- The product is defective upon arrival (e.g., non-functional electronics, damaged apparel stitching, items that do not match their description in any functional sense)
- The wrong item was shipped (SKU mismatch confirmed by warehouse fulfillment records)
- The item was damaged during transit and verified through photographic or carrier-provided evidence
- The item poses a safety hazard and must be returned for consumer protection reasons

Customers must report such issues within the relevant reporting window, which is typically 48 hours from the point of confirmed delivery. Failure to report within this window may invalidate the exception claim, although agents retain discretion to extend this period where delivery confirmation data is unreliable or delayed through no fault of the customer.

In all Final Sale exception cases, the escalation team must validate the exception before a refund is issued. Agents are not authorized to grant Final Sale exceptions unilaterally.

### Final Sale Interaction with Promotional Codes

When a Final Sale item is purchased using a promotional discount code or voucher, both the Final Sale policy and the promotions policy apply simultaneously. In cases of conflict, the most restrictive rule governs:

- The Final Sale non-return clause takes precedence over any general promotion refund provision
- The promotional discount does not create an entitlement to return that would otherwise not exist
- Refund amounts, if an exception is approved, are calculated based on the final price paid after any promotional discount

## Hygiene-Restricted Products

Products that fall under hygiene-sensitive categories—including but not limited to cosmetics, skincare items, personal grooming products, intimate apparel, and undergarments—are subject to strict non-return rules once opened or used.

This restriction exists primarily for public health reasons. Reselling or re-shelving opened hygiene products risks cross-contamination and violates health and safety standards applicable in most regions. The restriction is also required by multiple regulatory frameworks and must be enforced uniformly.

### Return eligibility requires all of the following:

- Original factory seal remains intact and unbroken
- No visible signs of use, application, or tampering
- Packaging in fully resalable condition, including all inserts, labels, and secondary packaging
- Return initiated within the standard return window (30 days from delivery for standard customers)

### Exceptions are allowed only when one of the following applies:

- Product is contaminated or defective upon arrival (e.g., foreign substances, mold, rancid odor before first use)
- Packaging is compromised prior to first use by the customer (e.g., broken seal upon opening outer box, leaking container)
- Customer received a product different from what was ordered (wrong formulation, wrong shade, incorrect variant)
- Product causes documented adverse reaction (subject to evidence requirements; customers may be asked to provide medical documentation for high-value claims)

These restrictions are enforced to comply with health and safety standards and to prevent cross-contamination risks. Regional exceptions may exist in jurisdictions where consumer law mandates broader return rights for defective goods regardless of category.

### Partial Seal or Partially Used Hygiene Products

If a customer claims a product is defective but the seal has been broken and the product has been partially used, the claim is evaluated on a case-by-case basis. Agents must consider:

- The nature of the defect (is it discoverable only through use?)
- Whether the level of use is consistent with a single-use assessment trial
- Customer history and any prior abuse indicators on the account

These cases may require photographs, batch number verification, or product recall checks before resolution.

## Perishable Goods Exception Integration

Perishable goods are governed by a stricter subset of exception rules. These items cannot be returned once delivered due to rapid degradation, food safety standards, and logistical impracticality of reverse-shipping perishables. This includes food items, fresh produce, chilled and frozen goods, fresh flowers, and similar temperature-sensitive products.

Refunds or replacements are considered only if one or more of the following conditions are met:

- The item arrives spoiled, melted, thawed, crushed, or otherwise unusable upon delivery
- Delivery delays directly contribute to product degradation beyond the usable state (i.e., the carrier or platform bears responsibility for the degradation)
- Packaging failure results in contamination, leakage, or structural damage to the product before its use-by date
- The item is missing from the delivery entirely (covered additionally by the missing items policy)
- The item received is materially different from what was ordered (wrong product, wrong weight class, wrong variety)

In such cases, customers must provide evidence within 24 hours of delivery. Evidence may include clear photographs of the product, timestamps, and delivery confirmation records. This 24-hour window is strictly enforced due to the nature of perishable degradation and the inability to assess condition after a delay.

This policy supersedes all general return provisions. Even if the customer is in an EU region that normally allows 14-day returns, perishable goods remain exempt from the right of withdrawal under applicable consumer law.

### Evidence Requirements for Perishable Claims

To expedite processing and ensure accurate decisions, customers submitting perishable damage claims should provide:

1. Clear photographic evidence of the damaged or unusable item, including the packaging
2. A description of the specific issue (e.g., "fully melted," "strong odor," "package leaking")
3. The delivery confirmation timestamp from the carrier notification or platform app
4. Any temperature indicators included in the packaging (e.g., frozen gel packs that are fully thawed)

Claims submitted without adequate evidence that cannot be verified through delivery records will be evaluated on a best-efforts basis. If verification is not possible, the claim may be escalated for manual review.

## Multi-Category Conflict Resolution

Products that fall under multiple restricted categories (e.g., a discounted perishable item marked as final sale, or an opened hygiene item that arrived damaged) are evaluated under the most restrictive applicable rule, unless an overriding exception condition is met.

### Examples of multi-category resolutions:

- Final sale + perishable → no return under any circumstances unless the item arrived defective or damaged, in which case the defective goods exception applies
- Hygiene + damaged → eligible only with verified evidence of pre-use damage or defect, assessed at agent or escalation level
- Perishable + marketplace seller → if the seller's policy is less restrictive than the platform perishable policy, the platform perishable policy takes precedence
- Final sale + hygiene → double non-returnable status; the most restrictive rule applies; no route to return exists unless a defect or fulfillment error is clearly established

Ambiguities arising from overlapping policies must be escalated for manual review. Agents must not make unilateral decisions in multi-category conflict cases.

## Opened Items Policy (General)

Beyond hygiene-specific restrictions, the platform applies a general policy to opened items across all categories:

- Items that are opened but otherwise in resalable condition may be accepted for return at the agent's discretion within the return window, subject to a restocking assessment
- Items that are clearly used, worn, altered, or damaged by the customer (beyond normal assessment) are not eligible for return
- Electronics that have been registered, activated, or configured may be subject to additional verification steps before return is accepted

In general, the burden of demonstrating that an item is in a returnable condition rests with the customer. Agents may request photographs, serial number verification, or similar confirmations as part of the assessment.

## Escalation Conditions and Review Protocol

Escalation is required when:

- Customer disputes classification of item category
- Evidence provided is inconclusive or conflicting
- Multiple policy rules create ambiguity
- A Final Sale exception is being considered
- Hygiene item has been partially used but customer claims defect was discoverable only through use
- High-value items (above $300 threshold) are involved in any exception category
- Customer explicitly references consumer protection law in support of their claim

Escalated cases are reviewed by specialized support teams who may override standard rules based on contextual judgment, regulatory requirements, or customer history. Final decisions from the escalation team are binding. They are documented in the case management system and referenced in any future interactions related to the same order.

## Customer Communication Standards

When denying returns under this policy, agents must:

- Clearly state the specific restriction that applies (Final Sale, hygiene, perishable)
- Provide a reference to the policy section
- Avoid language that implies the decision is reversible or subject to further negotiation unless an appeal mechanism is explicitly available
- Offer alternative resolution paths where available (e.g., store credit for partial goodwill, forward to escalation for defect claims)

Agents must not apologize for policy enforcement in a way that implies the policy is unfair. Empathy is appropriate; undermining policy is not.
