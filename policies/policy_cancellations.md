## Overview of Cancellation Rights

Customers may request cancellation of an order depending on its processing stage. Cancellation eligibility is strictly tied to fulfillment status, system processing speed, and item category. The platform aims to provide flexibility while ensuring operational efficiency and preventing misuse of the cancellation mechanism.

Order cancellations interact closely with the refunds, returns, and payment policies. This document should be read alongside those policies for a complete understanding of the cancellation lifecycle. Any decision involving cancellation of a marketplace seller order must additionally account for the seller policy framework.

---

## Pre-Shipment Cancellation

Orders that have not yet entered the shipping phase are eligible for cancellation. The following conditions apply:

- Orders in "processing," "confirmed," or "placed" state may be canceled through official support channels or the self-service order management interface
- Cancellation must be initiated through official channels (support ticket, app, or website — not informal communication channels)
- Refunds are issued to the original payment method unless that method is no longer valid

### Refund timelines for pre-shipment cancellations:

- Refund initiation: immediate upon cancellation approval by the system
- Processing time: 3–5 business days for standard payment methods
- Credit card refunds: may require additional 2–3 business days depending on card issuer
- Digital wallets (PayPal, Apple Pay, etc.): typically processed within 24–48 hours
- Store credit alternatives: issued immediately if customer opts in

Certain high-demand or fast-moving items may transition rapidly from "placed" to "shipped" status, limiting the effective cancellation window. Customers placing orders for such items should act promptly if cancellation is desired.

---

## Post-Shipment Cancellation Restrictions

Once an order is marked as "shipped," cancellation is no longer possible through standard mechanisms. Customers must instead follow the return process after taking delivery of the item. This policy ensures logistical consistency and prevents disruption to carrier workflows, which can cause delays for other customers.

The "shipped" status is defined as the point at which a carrier tracking number has been assigned and the package has been physically inducted into the carrier network. It does not refer to the internal warehouse processing stage.

### If a customer refuses delivery of a shipped order:

- The order will be treated as a return shipment
- The package must reach the platform's returns processing center before any refund is initiated
- Refunds are subject to return inspection policies, including deductions for damaged or missing items
- Return shipping costs for refused deliveries may be charged to the customer unless the refusal is due to a fulfillment error (e.g., wrong item, clearly damaged package)

Customers are strongly advised against refusing delivery as a cancellation workaround, as it may delay refund processing by several weeks and may not protect against applicable restocking fees.

---

## Instant Processing Orders

Orders labeled as "instant processing" or "express fulfillment" are processed immediately upon placement due to automation within warehouse management systems. These orders cannot be canceled once confirmed, regardless of the time elapsed since placement.

### Categories typically subject to instant processing:

- Same-day delivery items (scheduled delivery slots that have already been dispatched to a courier)
- Digital goods, software licenses, and downloadable content (non-cancellable once access is granted)
- Gift cards and prepaid codes (non-cancellable once issued)
- High-priority logistics items fulfilled from time-sensitive inventory
- Pre-ordered items whose release date has passed and fulfillment has begun

Customers are advised to review product listings for instant processing indicators before placing their orders. These items are clearly marked on the product detail page.

---

## Partial Cancellation of Multi-Item Orders

For orders containing multiple items, partial cancellation may be possible under the following conditions:

- Individual items that have not yet been picked, packed, or shipped may be canceled independently
- Items that have already been inducted into the shipping process cannot be individually canceled
- Mixed orders (some items shipped, some not) will result in partial order modification rather than full cancellation

### Refund calculation for partial cancellations:

Refunds are calculated proportionally based on the canceled item's price at time of purchase, inclusive of any item-specific discounts. Order-level discounts (e.g., free shipping thresholds, bundle pricing) may be recalculated if partial cancellation causes the order to fall below a qualifying threshold.

For example, if an order qualified for free shipping because its total exceeded a threshold, and a partial cancellation reduces the total below that threshold, applicable shipping charges may be re-applied and deducted from the refund.

## Payment and Refund Constraints

Refunds for cancellations are issued to the original payment method wherever technically possible. In cases where:

- The payment method is no longer valid (e.g., expired card, closed account)
- The refund fails due to banking or payment processor issues after three automated attempts
- The payment was made through a method that does not support automated refunds

Alternative methods such as store credit, platform wallet credit, or manual wire transfer (for high-value amounts above a threshold) may be used. Customers must proactively contact support if they are aware that their original payment method is invalid.

## Digital Goods and Non-Returnable Cancellations

Digital products, streaming access, and software licenses present unique cancellation challenges:

- Cancellation is not permitted once access or download has been initiated
- In jurisdictions where consumer law requires a cancellation right for digital goods (e.g., EU Directive 2019/770), the right is waived upon download or first access, provided the customer gave explicit consent to this waiver at checkout
- Exceptions may be considered in cases of proven technical failure that prevented access to the purchased product

## Edge Cases and Exceptions

Cancellation requests that overlap with shipment dispatch may result in ambiguous system states. In such cases:

- The platform will attempt to intercept the shipment at the carrier level, which succeeds in approximately 30–40% of cases depending on carrier type and geographic location
- If interception fails, the customer is directed to the return process post-delivery
- Refund timelines in interception scenarios may be extended by 3–7 business days pending confirmation of package diversion

Other edge cases include:

- Duplicate orders: automatically detected and the later order canceled; refunds processed within standard timelines
- Orders placed during system outages or pricing errors: the platform reserves the right to cancel and refund such orders even without customer request
- Subscription order cancellations: governed by the separate subscription policy and may require address to the subscription management portal

---

## Escalation Conditions

Escalation is required when:

- System status conflicts with actual shipment state (e.g., system shows "placed" but carrier already has the package)
- Customer disputes cancellation eligibility based on timing
- Payment refund has not been received within the stated processing window
- Cancellation affects a high-value order (above $500 threshold)
- Customer is in an EU jurisdiction and references statutory rights related to cancellation
- Customer has initiated a chargeback before completing the standard cancellation process

---

## Agent Guidelines for Cancellation Decisions

When handling cancellation requests, agents must:

1. Verify the current fulfillment status using the internal order management tool before making any commitments
2. Confirm whether the item category is subject to instant processing exclusions
3. Calculate refund amounts accurately, including shipping and applicable discounts
4. Document the cancellation reason in the case notes for quality assurance purposes
5. Not promise interception outcomes that cannot be guaranteed by the carrier system
6. Refer marketplace seller order cancellations to the appropriate seller escalation pathway

### Appendix A: Detailed Application Guidelines and Training Scenarios In order to ensure that all agents apply this policy correctly across diverse customer scenarios, the following detailed guidelines and case studies have been appended to this document. These scenarios are drawn from historical support logs and represent common edge cases where policy application requires nuanced judgment. It is imperative that all support staff review these scenarios regularly. The consistent application of policy is the foundation of our customer trust and regulatory compliance program. 1. **Scenario Assessment Methodology:** Before reviewing historical cases, agents must understand the core methodology for scenario assessment. First, immediately establish the factual timeline of the customer interaction. This means reviewing all system timestamps, starting from the exact moment of order placement, up to the precise second the carrier logged a delivery event. Do not rely on customer accounts of timeframes if they conflict with system logs. Second, review the customer’s interaction history. Are there past instances where similar policies were applied? If so, continuity in decision-making is preferred, unless the previous decision was explicitly marked as an error in the quality assurance (QA) audit. Third, consult the inventory management system to confirm the exact SKU and its assigned categorization at the time the order was placed. Categories can occasionally shift due to catalog updates; the category active at the time of purchase is the governing one. 2. **Cross-Departmental Coordination Protocol:** Often, policy resolution requires input from departments outside standard tier-1 support. For example, involving the logistics liaison team is necessary when carrier tracking states a package was delivered, but GPS coordinates suggest misdelivery to a neighboring postal code. In such cases, agents must open a sub-ticket tagged for the logistics team and place the main customer interaction in a pending state for no more than 48 hours. When coordinating with the finance department regarding complex refund routing (e.g., split payments involving store credit and an expired credit card), agents must provide the exact transaction ledger IDs. Do not attempt to manually calculate prorated refunds for multi-item orders with partial cancellations; always use the automated refund calculation tool. 3. **Regulatory Audit Readiness:** It is critical to recognize that every ticket resolved under this policy may be subject to external regulatory audit, particularly in jurisdictions with robust consumer protection authorities. Therefore, the case notes must be written in clear, professional language that explicitly cites the relevant section of this policy document. For example, rather than writing "Denied return because item is opened," agents must write "Return request denied pursuant to Section 3 of the Hygiene-Restricted Products policy, as photographic evidence confirmed the factory seal was broken." This level of precision protects the platform during compliance reviews. 4. **Handling Customer Escalation Threats:** Customers may occasionally threaten to involve third-party dispute resolution services, credit card chargebacks, or social media exposure when a policy is enforced against their favor. Agents must remain objective and de-escalate without compromising the integrity of the policy. The standard response should calmly reiterate the policy provision, explain the rationale behind it, and outline the formal internal appeals process if one applies. Agents are authorized to offer a one-time goodwill credit of up to $15 to de-escalate tense situations, provided the customer account is in good standing and the interaction does not involve suspected fraud. This goodwill credit must not be framed as an admission of fault or a reversal of the policy decision. 5. **System Downtime Contingencies:** In the event that the primary order management system experiences degraded performance or a full outage, agents must not issue definitive resolutions based on incomplete cached data. Instead, invoke the standard downtime communication protocol, informing the customer that their request has been logged and will be processed immediately upon system restoration. If a policy deadline (e.g., the end of a 30-day return window) expires during a system outage, the system will automatically grant a grace period equivalent to the duration of the outage plus 24 hours. Agents manually reviewing such cases must apply this grace period proactively. 6. **Continuous Improvement Feedback Loop:** The contents of this policy are subject to quarterly review by the Global Policy Committee. Agents who identify recurring customer friction points or ambiguities in the text are encouraged to submit annotated examples through the internal policy feedback portal.
