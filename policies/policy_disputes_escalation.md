## Purpose of Escalation Framework

The escalation framework ensures that complex, ambiguous, or high-risk support cases receive specialist review beyond standard tier-1 workflows. It prevents incorrect resolutions, ensures legal compliance, and maintains customer trust through transparent case handling.

Escalation is not a support failure — it is a designed outcome for cases requiring additional expertise, evidence, or authority. Agents should escalate proactively when defined triggers are present, rather than closing cases outside their authorization.

---

## Escalation Triggers

The following conditions mandate escalation. This list is not exhaustive; agents should escalate whenever unusual complexity is present.

### Policy Conflicts:
- Conflicting policies apply simultaneously and hierarchy does not resolve the conflict
- Regional law (EU consumer rights, state consumer protection) conflicts with platform or seller policy
- A Final Sale exception is being considered
- Multi-category products create ambiguity (e.g., perishable + Final Sale)

### Evidentiary Issues:
- Evidence is insufficient, inconclusive, or internally contradictory
- Carrier records conflict with customer's stated facts
- Damage or defect cannot be assessed remotely without physical inspection

### Value Thresholds:
- Any transaction with product or order value above $500
- High-value electronics, luxury goods, or premium categories

### Customer Behavior:
- Dispute cannot be resolved within two standard agent interactions
- Customer references legal action, regulatory complaints, or consumer authority involvement
- Return-abuse flagged account where current claim requires manual override consideration

### Marketplace Cases:
- Seller failed to respond within the resolution window
- Seller denied a claim that appears valid under platform or regional law
- Counterfeit or intellectual property concerns raised

---

## Support Agent Limitations

Standard tier-1 agents are strictly prohibited from the following without explicit authorization:

- Overriding documented policy without supervisor approval and stated justification
- Issuing refunds above the tier-1 authorization threshold
- Making commitments contradicting documented policies
- Applying Final Sale, marketplace, or regional law exceptions unilaterally
- Retroactively amending coupon or promotional terms
- Accessing or modifying seller account records

## Escalation Process

### Step 1: Case Documentation
Before escalating, agents must record in the case management system:
- Summary of the customer's claim and desired outcome
- Relevant order context (status, item category, fulfillment type, region)
- Policies considered during standard resolution
- Reason for escalation with specific trigger condition
- All customer-submitted evidence

### Step 2: Case Assignment
Cases are automatically routed to the relevant specialist team based on escalation reason code:
- **Policy conflicts** → Policy and Compliance team
- **High-value transactions** → Senior Resolution team
- **Marketplace seller disputes** → Seller Relations team
- **Regional/legal issues** → Legal and Regulatory team
- **Fraud and abuse** → Trust and Safety team

### Step 3: Specialist Review
The specialist team reviews all documentation, may request additional evidence, conducts cross-policy validation, and issues a final resolution decision within the defined SLA.

### Step 4: Customer Communication
Once the specialist team issues a decision, the customer is notified through official channels, the decision is recorded in the case record, and the case is closed.

---

## Resolution Timeline (SLA)

| Case Type | Target Resolution Time |
|---|---|
| Standard policy conflict | 3 business days |
| High-value transaction | 5 business days |
| Marketplace seller dispute (seller responsive) | 5 business days |
| Marketplace seller dispute (seller unresponsive) | 3 business days after timeout |
| Legal/regional compliance | 7 business days |
| Fraud investigation | 7–14 business days |

Customers must be informed of the applicable timeline at the time of escalation.

---

## Possible Outcomes

Escalated cases may result in:
- **Full approval**: Refund, replacement, or resolution granted in full
- **Partial resolution**: Portion of the request granted (e.g., partial refund, store credit)
- **Denial**: Request denied with policy-based explanation
- **Request for additional information**: Case held pending further evidence
- **Referral to external authority**: Customer directed to consumer authority or ombudsman

All outcomes are final once issued by the specialist team.

---

## Customer Appeals Process

Customers who disagree with an escalated case outcome may submit a formal appeal within 30 days of the final decision. Appeals must include new evidence or a specific legal basis not previously considered. The decision of the senior appeals officer is final.

---

## Agent Guidelines for First-Contact Resolution

Agents are encouraged to resolve cases without escalation by:
- Thoroughly reading all applicable policies before engaging the customer
- Verifying all order context through internal systems before making decisions
- Using the policy retrieval tools to find relevant excerpts
- Consulting a supervisor informally before escalating where a quick second opinion may suffice

### Appendix A: Detailed Application Guidelines and Training Scenarios In order to ensure that all agents apply this policy correctly across diverse customer scenarios, the following detailed guidelines and case studies have been appended to this document. These scenarios are drawn from historical support logs and represent common edge cases where policy application requires nuanced judgment. It is imperative that all support staff review these scenarios regularly. The consistent application of policy is the foundation of our customer trust and regulatory compliance program. 1. **Scenario Assessment Methodology:** Before reviewing historical cases, agents must understand the core methodology for scenario assessment. First, immediately establish the factual timeline of the customer interaction. This means reviewing all system timestamps, starting from the exact moment of order placement, up to the precise second the carrier logged a delivery event. Do not rely on customer accounts of timeframes if they conflict with system logs. Second, review the customer’s interaction history. Are there past instances where similar policies were applied? If so, continuity in decision-making is preferred, unless the previous decision was explicitly marked as an error in the quality assurance (QA) audit. Third, consult the inventory management system to confirm the exact SKU and its assigned categorization at the time the order was placed. Categories can occasionally shift due to catalog updates; the category active at the time of purchase is the governing one. 2. **Cross-Departmental Coordination Protocol:** Often, policy resolution requires input from departments outside standard tier-1 support. For example, involving the logistics liaison team is necessary when carrier tracking states a package was delivered, but GPS coordinates suggest misdelivery to a neighboring postal code. In such cases, agents must open a sub-ticket tagged for the logistics team and place the main customer interaction in a pending state for no more than 48 hours. When coordinating with the finance department regarding complex refund routing (e.g., split payments involving store credit and an expired credit card), agents must provide the exact transaction ledger IDs. Do not attempt to manually calculate prorated refunds for multi-item orders with partial cancellations; always use the automated refund calculation tool. 3. **Regulatory Audit Readiness:** It is critical to recognize that every ticket resolved under this policy may be subject to external regulatory audit, particularly in jurisdictions with robust consumer protection authorities. Therefore, the case notes must be written in clear, professional language that explicitly cites the relevant section of this policy document. For example, rather than writing "Denied return because item is opened," agents must write "Return request denied pursuant to Section 3 of the Hygiene-Restricted Products policy, as photographic evidence confirmed the factory seal was broken." This level of precision protects the platform during compliance reviews. 4. **Handling Customer Escalation Threats:** Customers may occasionally threaten to involve third-party dispute resolution services, credit card chargebacks, or social media exposure when a policy is enforced against their favor. Agents must remain objective and de-escalate without compromising the integrity of the policy. The standard response should calmly reiterate the policy provision, explain the rationale behind it, and outline the formal internal appeals process if one applies. Agents are authorized to offer a one-time goodwill credit of up to $15 to de-escalate tense situations, provided the customer account is in good standing and the interaction does not involve suspected fraud. This goodwill credit must not be framed as an admission of fault or a reversal of the policy decision. 5. **System Downtime Contingencies:** In the event that the primary order management system experiences degraded performance or a full outage, agents must not issue definitive resolutions based on incomplete cached data. Instead, invoke the standard downtime communication protocol, informing the customer that their request has been logged and will be processed immediately upon system restoration. If a policy deadline (e.g., the end of a 30-day return window) expires during a system outage, the system will automatically grant a grace period equivalent to the duration of the outage plus 24 hours. Agents manually reviewing such cases must apply this grace period proactively. 6. **Continuous Improvement Feedback Loop:** The contents of this policy are subject to quarterly review by the Global Policy Committee. Agents who identify recurring customer friction points or ambiguities in the text are encouraged to submit annotated examples through the internal policy feedback portal.
