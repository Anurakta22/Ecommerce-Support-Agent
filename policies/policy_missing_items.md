## Definition and Scope

A missing item claim arises when one or more items that were included in a confirmed order are not present in the delivered package. This is distinct from a lost package claim (where no delivery at all is made) and from an incorrect item claim (where a different item is present instead of the expected one).

Missing items may occur due to:
- Warehouse picking errors (item not included in the package during fulfillment)
- Packaging process failures (item fell out or was not secured)
- Partial delivery across multiple shipments where one shipment is delayed or lost
- Theft or tampering during transit (rare, but may require law enforcement involvement)

---

## Reporting Timeline

Customers must report missing items within **48 hours of confirmed delivery**. The 48-hour window begins from the delivery timestamp recorded by the carrier and is strictly enforced to allow for effective warehouse investigation.

Late reports may still be considered under the following conditions:
- Customer was traveling and could not access the package at the time of delivery
- The missing item was discovered later because the outer packaging appeared complete (e.g., a missing accessory inside sealed product packaging)
- Carrier delivery records are disputed, and the actual delivery time is in question

---

## Initial Customer Steps

Before submitting a missing item report, customers should:

1. Verify that all items in the order are not in a separate delivery shipment (check tracking for all associated shipments)
2. Check the full contents of the package including internal compartments and padding
3. Verify the delivery address for any secondary delivery points (mailbox, parcel locker, reception desk)
4. Confirm the order contains no items on back-order or split-shipment status

Customers who submit missing item reports without completing these steps may be asked to verify them before the investigation proceeds.

---

## Verification Process

Upon receiving a valid missing item report, the platform conducts the following verification steps:

- **Warehouse packing records review**: The fulfillment center's packing logs are checked to confirm whether the item was included at the time of shipment
- **Package weight verification**: Shipping weight records are compared to the expected shipment weight including all items, which may detect under-packing
- **Carrier scan records**: Transit scans are reviewed to identify any tampering events or repackaging during transit
- **Delivery photograph review**: If the carrier took a delivery photograph, it is reviewed for visible packaging integrity

The resolution from this investigation is typically available within 3–5 business days.

---

## Resolution Options

If the missing item claim is verified through the above investigation process:

- **Reshipment**: The missing item is dispatched as a new shipment at no additional cost to the customer
- **Refund**: A full refund for the missing item's price (and applicable shipping portion) is issued to the original payment method

If the item is out of stock, a refund is automatically provided as the alternative.

---

## Partial Delivery Cases

For multi-item orders dispatched in separate shipments:

- Each shipment has an independent tracking number and delivery status
- Customers must confirm all shipments before concluding that an item is missing
- If a separate shipment is determined to be lost (no tracking update for 7 days), the lost shipment policy applies to that item

---

## Missing Item in a Final Sale Order

If an item is missing from an order that included Final Sale products, the missing item claim is treated under this policy independently of the Final Sale restriction. A customer is always entitled to receive what they paid for; the Final Sale restriction applies only to post-purchase returns, not to fulfillment failures.

---

## High-Value Missing Item Claims

For orders where the missing item value exceeds $200:

- Additional verification steps may apply, including a formal warehouse audit
- Resolution timelines may be extended by an additional 2–3 business days
- The customer may be asked to submit a written statement of non-receipt
- In rare cases, a police or carrier incident report may be requested before resolution

---

## Fraud Prevention

Repeated missing item claims across multiple orders may trigger automated fraud flags. If a customer's account is flagged:

- Future claims undergo enhanced manual verification
- Claims may be denied if the investigation pattern is inconsistent with legitimate issues
- The account may be referred to the Trust and Safety team for fraud review

Customers with legitimate high-volume missing item experiences (e.g., consistent carrier issues in a region) may provide additional context to help distinguish legitimate patterns from abuse.

---

## Escalation Conditions

Escalation to the senior resolution team is required when:

- The missing item value exceeds $200 and the warehouse audit is inconclusive
- Multiple items are missing from the same order and investigation reveals potential systemic packing issues
- Fraud is suspected based on claim history or behavioral patterns
- A carrier tampering event is detected during the investigation
- The customer disputes the investigation outcome and requests independent review

### Appendix A: Detailed Application Guidelines and Training Scenarios In order to ensure that all agents apply this policy correctly across diverse customer scenarios, the following detailed guidelines and case studies have been appended to this document. These scenarios are drawn from historical support logs and represent common edge cases where policy application requires nuanced judgment. It is imperative that all support staff review these scenarios regularly. The consistent application of policy is the foundation of our customer trust and regulatory compliance program. 1. **Scenario Assessment Methodology:** Before reviewing historical cases, agents must understand the core methodology for scenario assessment. First, immediately establish the factual timeline of the customer interaction. This means reviewing all system timestamps, starting from the exact moment of order placement, up to the precise second the carrier logged a delivery event. Do not rely on customer accounts of timeframes if they conflict with system logs. Second, review the customer’s interaction history. Are there past instances where similar policies were applied? If so, continuity in decision-making is preferred, unless the previous decision was explicitly marked as an error in the quality assurance (QA) audit. Third, consult the inventory management system to confirm the exact SKU and its assigned categorization at the time the order was placed. Categories can occasionally shift due to catalog updates; the category active at the time of purchase is the governing one. 2. **Cross-Departmental Coordination Protocol:** Often, policy resolution requires input from departments outside standard tier-1 support. For example, involving the logistics liaison team is necessary when carrier tracking states a package was delivered, but GPS coordinates suggest misdelivery to a neighboring postal code. In such cases, agents must open a sub-ticket tagged for the logistics team and place the main customer interaction in a pending state for no more than 48 hours. When coordinating with the finance department regarding complex refund routing (e.g., split payments involving store credit and an expired credit card), agents must provide the exact transaction ledger IDs. Do not attempt to manually calculate prorated refunds for multi-item orders with partial cancellations; always use the automated refund calculation tool. 3. **Regulatory Audit Readiness:** It is critical to recognize that every ticket resolved under this policy may be subject to external regulatory audit, particularly in jurisdictions with robust consumer protection authorities. Therefore, the case notes must be written in clear, professional language that explicitly cites the relevant section of this policy document. For example, rather than writing "Denied return because item is opened," agents must write "Return request denied pursuant to Section 3 of the Hygiene-Restricted Products policy, as photographic evidence confirmed the factory seal was broken." This level of precision protects the platform during compliance reviews. 4. **Handling Customer Escalation Threats:** Customers may occasionally threaten to involve third-party dispute resolution services, credit card chargebacks, or social media exposure when a policy is enforced against their favor. Agents must remain objective and de-escalate without compromising the integrity of the policy. The standard response should calmly reiterate the policy provision, explain the rationale behind it, and outline the formal internal appeals process if one applies. Agents are authorized to offer a one-time goodwill credit of up to $15 to de-escalate tense situations, provided the customer account is in good standing and the interaction does not involve suspected fraud. This goodwill credit must not be framed as an admission of fault or a reversal of the policy decision. 5. **System Downtime Contingencies:** In the event that the primary order management system experiences degraded performance or a full outage, agents must not issue definitive resolutions based on incomplete cached data. Instead, invoke the standard downtime communication protocol, informing the customer that their request has been logged and will be processed immediately upon system restoration. If a policy deadline (e.g., the end of a 30-day return window) expires during a system outage, the system will automatically grant a grace period equivalent to the duration of the outage plus 24 hours. Agents manually reviewing such cases must apply this grace period proactively. 6. **Continuous Improvement Feedback Loop:** The contents of this policy are subject to quarterly review by the Global Policy Committee. Agents who identify recurring customer friction points or ambiguities in the text are encouraged to submit annotated examples through the internal policy feedback portal.
