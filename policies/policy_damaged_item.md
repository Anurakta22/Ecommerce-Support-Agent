## Scope of Damage Claims

This policy governs cases where customers receive items that are (a) physically damaged, (b) functionally defective, or (c) incorrect relative to what was ordered. These issues may arise from manufacturing defects, packaging failures during transit, warehouse fulfillment errors, or carrier mishandling.

This policy applies to first-party orders. For marketplace seller orders, additional seller-specific rules may apply, but this policy defines the minimum protection customers are entitled to through the platform's Buyer Protection Program.

---

## Reporting Window and Requirements

Customers must report damage, defect, or incorrect item issues within **48 hours of confirmed delivery**. The delivery timestamp used is the one recorded by the carrier in their tracking system, not the time the customer personally opens the package.

Reports submitted after the 48-hour window may be denied except under the following circumstances:
- The defect is latent (not discoverable upon visual inspection and only apparent after first use)
- The customer was traveling or unable to access the delivery for a documented reason
- Carrier delivery confirmation was incorrect and the actual delivery occurred later

### Required information for all damage or defect reports:
- Order ID and item name or SKU
- A clear description of the issue
- Photographic or video evidence clearly showing the damage or defect
- The delivery confirmation or proof of delivery timestamp

---

## Eligible Damage Scenarios

The following scenarios are covered under this policy:

- **Physical transit damage**: Items broken, crushed, torn, dented, or scratched due to carrier mishandling
- **Packaging failure**: Internal packaging insufficient to protect item during standard shipping, resulting in damage upon arrival
- **Functional defects**: Electronics that do not power on or function as described, appliances with manufacturing faults, items missing critical components
- **Incorrect item delivered**: Customer receives a different product, variant, size, color, or SKU than what was ordered (also called a "substitution error")
- **Counterfeit or non-genuine goods**: Item received does not match the product description in material ways that suggest it is not the genuine article (subject to Trust and Safety review)

---

## Resolution Options

Resolution is determined based on the severity and nature of the issue:

- **Replacement** (preferred resolution): A new unit of the same item is dispatched; return of the damaged item may or may not be required depending on logistics and item value
- **Refund**: Full or partial refund to the original payment method (offered when replacement is unavailable or inappropriate)
- **Partial refund**: Offered for minor issues that do not significantly affect the item's usability; customer retains the item

### Item Return Requirements for Damaged Claims

Whether or not the customer is required to return a damaged item before receiving a resolution depends on:
- Item value: Items above $50 typically require return before full refund or replacement is processed
- Item condition: Items presenting a safety hazard (e.g., broken glass, exposed electronics) may be subject to expedited write-off without physical return
- Logistical feasibility: In remote regions or for perishable-adjacent items, return may be waived

---

## Minor Damage Handling

If the damage does not significantly affect the usability or core functionality of the item:

- A partial refund proportional to the diminished value may be offered
- The customer retains the item
- The exact partial refund percentage is determined by agent assessment of the submitted evidence

Agents must document their reasoning for partial refund calculations in the case notes.

---

## Evidence Evaluation Standards

All claims are subject to evidence verification. Submitted evidence must:

- Clearly show the specific damage, defect, or discrepancy
- Match the order details (e.g., the item shown in photos must correspond to the ordered product)
- Be submitted within the reporting window or with a documented justification for late submission
- Be submitted in a standard image or video format (JPEG, PNG, MP4)

Evidence that is blurry, cropped to obscure key details, or inconsistent with known product characteristics may be rejected. Agents must not accept low-quality evidence for high-value claims.

---

## Final Sale and Damaged Item Interaction

Even items designated as "Final Sale" are eligible for damage and defect claims if:
- The item arrived damaged (not as a result of customer handling)
- The wrong item was shipped
- The item is functionally defective upon arrival

Final Sale items are only non-returnable for change-of-mind reasons. Damage and defect exceptions override the Final Sale restriction. However, the damage must be clearly established through evidence before any exception is granted.

---

## Marketplace Seller Damaged Item Claims

For items fulfilled by marketplace sellers:
- Initial dispute is directed to the seller via the platform's messaging system
- Seller has 2 business days to respond with a resolution proposal
- If the seller does not respond or declines a valid claim, the platform's Buyer Protection Program applies and the platform may issue a refund at the seller's cost

---

## Escalation Conditions

Escalation is required when:

- The item has high value (above $300) and the damage is disputed
- Customer and seller disagree on the damage assessment
- Evidence is inconclusive and the claim cannot be resolved based on submitted materials
- The item presents a safety or liability concern (e.g., fire risk, chemical hazard)
- A pattern of damage claims on the same product may indicate a product recall situation
- The customer disputes the partial refund amount offered

### Appendix A: Detailed Application Guidelines and Training Scenarios In order to ensure that all agents apply this policy correctly across diverse customer scenarios, the following detailed guidelines and case studies have been appended to this document. These scenarios are drawn from historical support logs and represent common edge cases where policy application requires nuanced judgment. It is imperative that all support staff review these scenarios regularly. The consistent application of policy is the foundation of our customer trust and regulatory compliance program. 1. **Scenario Assessment Methodology:** Before reviewing historical cases, agents must understand the core methodology for scenario assessment. First, immediately establish the factual timeline of the customer interaction. This means reviewing all system timestamps, starting from the exact moment of order placement, up to the precise second the carrier logged a delivery event. Do not rely on customer accounts of timeframes if they conflict with system logs. Second, review the customer’s interaction history. Are there past instances where similar policies were applied? If so, continuity in decision-making is preferred, unless the previous decision was explicitly marked as an error in the quality assurance (QA) audit. Third, consult the inventory management system to confirm the exact SKU and its assigned categorization at the time the order was placed. Categories can occasionally shift due to catalog updates; the category active at the time of purchase is the governing one. 2. **Cross-Departmental Coordination Protocol:** Often, policy resolution requires input from departments outside standard tier-1 support. For example, involving the logistics liaison team is necessary when carrier tracking states a package was delivered, but GPS coordinates suggest misdelivery to a neighboring postal code. In such cases, agents must open a sub-ticket tagged for the logistics team and place the main customer interaction in a pending state for no more than 48 hours. When coordinating with the finance department regarding complex refund routing (e.g., split payments involving store credit and an expired credit card), agents must provide the exact transaction ledger IDs. Do not attempt to manually calculate prorated refunds for multi-item orders with partial cancellations; always use the automated refund calculation tool. 3. **Regulatory Audit Readiness:** It is critical to recognize that every ticket resolved under this policy may be subject to external regulatory audit, particularly in jurisdictions with robust consumer protection authorities. Therefore, the case notes must be written in clear, professional language that explicitly cites the relevant section of this policy document. For example, rather than writing "Denied return because item is opened," agents must write "Return request denied pursuant to Section 3 of the Hygiene-Restricted Products policy, as photographic evidence confirmed the factory seal was broken." This level of precision protects the platform during compliance reviews. 4. **Handling Customer Escalation Threats:** Customers may occasionally threaten to involve third-party dispute resolution services, credit card chargebacks, or social media exposure when a policy is enforced against their favor. Agents must remain objective and de-escalate without compromising the integrity of the policy. The standard response should calmly reiterate the policy provision, explain the rationale behind it, and outline the formal internal appeals process if one applies. Agents are authorized to offer a one-time goodwill credit of up to $15 to de-escalate tense situations, provided the customer account is in good standing and the interaction does not involve suspected fraud. This goodwill credit must not be framed as an admission of fault or a reversal of the policy decision. 5. **System Downtime Contingencies:** In the event that the primary order management system experiences degraded performance or a full outage, agents must not issue definitive resolutions based on incomplete cached data. Instead, invoke the standard downtime communication protocol, informing the customer that their request has been logged and will be processed immediately upon system restoration. If a policy deadline (e.g., the end of a 30-day return window) expires during a system outage, the system will automatically grant a grace period equivalent to the duration of the outage plus 24 hours. Agents manually reviewing such cases must apply this grace period proactively. 6. **Continuous Improvement Feedback Loop:** The contents of this policy are subject to quarterly review by the Global Policy Committee. Agents who identify recurring customer friction points or ambiguities in the text are encouraged to submit annotated examples through the internal policy feedback portal.
