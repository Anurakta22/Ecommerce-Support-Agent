## General Return Framework for US Customers

US customers are covered by the platform's standard return and refund policy unless a state-specific provision creates additional obligations. Unlike the European Union's statutory withdrawal rights, the United States does not have a federal law mandating return rights for online purchases. Instead, consumers rely on the company's voluntary return policy, the terms of purchase, and applicable state regulations for specific categories.

The platform's standard US return window is **30 days from the date of delivery** for most eligible product categories. Returns must be initiated through official channels—the platform's return portal, the mobile app, or through a support ticket—before the 30-day window expires. Items received after the 30-day window without prior authorization may be refused.

## State-Level Variations and Consumer Protection

While US federal law does not mandate return policies for most e-commerce transactions, certain states impose additional consumer protection requirements. Agents should be aware of the following common state-level considerations:

### California

- California's Consumer Legal Remedies Act (CLRA) provides additional protections against deceptive practices in product descriptions and returns
- If the platform's policy is characterized as allowing returns, a seller may be legally required to honor it under California consumer protection law even if technical eligibility conditions are not met
- California customers may also be eligible for additional remedies in cases of defective goods under implied warranty provisions

### New York

- New York requires that return and refund policies be clearly posted at the point of sale. If no policy is posted or the policy is unclear, the state's default refund policy (30 days for full refund) may apply
- Price scanning errors may entitle customers to the scanned price in jurisdictions that enforce scanner accuracy laws

### Other States

- Some states impose specific cooling-off periods for door-to-door sales or home solicitation purchases, which are not typically applicable to online retail but may arise in edge cases involving third-party seller direct outreach
- State lemon laws covering defective new vehicles do not apply to the platform's product range, but analogous protections may exist for high-value electronics in some jurisdictions

When a customer specifically invokes state consumer protection law, the case must be escalated for review before the standard policy is enforced. Agents should not unilaterally apply state law without guidance from the compliance team.

## Standard Eligibility Conditions

For a US customer to be eligible for a return and refund under the platform's standard policy, all of the following conditions must be met:

1. The return is initiated within 30 days of the confirmed delivery date
2. The item is in its original or near-original condition, subject to category-specific rules
3. The return reason is valid under the applicable policy (standard change-of-mind, defective, or incorrect item)
4. The item is not in an excluded category (Final Sale, perishables, opened hygiene, digital goods)
5. The return is processed through the official return portal or authorized support channel

### Condition Assessment

Returned items undergo a physical inspection upon arrival at the returns center. Items that do not meet condition requirements may result in:

- Partial refund proportional to diminished condition
- Return of the item to the customer at their expense
- Full denial of refund in cases of significant damage attributable to customer use

## Refund Processing Rules for US Customers

Refunds are processed after successful completion of the return shipment receipt and inspection. Standard timelines:

- Refund initiation: 1–2 business days after inspection approval
- Credit card refund processing: 3–7 business days depending on issuing bank
- Debit card refund: 3–5 business days
- PayPal and digital wallets: 24–48 hours
- Store credit: Immediate upon approval

Customers who believe their refund has not been received after the maximum processing window should contact support with their order ID and payment confirmation. Do not dispute the charge with the bank before confirming the refund status with the platform — chargebacks initiated before resolution may complicate the process.

## Exceptions and Restricted Categories for US Customers

The following categories are subject to standard exception restrictions in the US context:

- **Final Sale items**: Non-returnable unless defective or incorrectly shipped
- **Opened hygiene products**: Non-returnable unless defective or damaged upon arrival
- **Perishable goods**: Non-returnable; refund or replacement only for items that arrived in an unusable state
- **Digital content**: Non-refundable once downloaded or accessed
- **Gift cards and prepaid codes**: Non-refundable once delivered or activated
- **Customized items**: Non-refundable unless manufacturing error is confirmed

## Price Matching Policy

The platform does not offer post-purchase price matching to competitor pricing. If a product purchased from the platform subsequently becomes available at a lower price at a third-party retailer:

- The platform will not issue a partial refund or credit to match that price
- The customer may return the item within the standard return window (if eligible) and repurchase at the lower price after
- This policy is final and applies uniformly across all US customer segments

### Platform Price Drops

If the platform's own listed price for a product drops after a customer's purchase, price adjustment requests may be considered within **7 days of the original purchase date**, subject to the following conditions:

- The item has not yet been shipped or is within the cancellation window
- The price drop is not attributable to a flash sale, limited-time promotion, or coupon code event
- The request is submitted within the 7-day window through official channels

Price adjustments for already-delivered orders are not available under the standard policy but may be considered as a goodwill gesture for high-value customers at agent or supervisor discretion.

## Conflict Handling and Policy Hierarchy

Platform policy applies to US customers unless:

- A specific state law creates an overriding obligation
- A contractual obligation from a seller's terms creates a more favorable right for the customer
- A documented promotional commitment creates an explicit exception

In all other cases, the platform's standard US policy governs. Conflicts between platform policy and state law must be escalated.

---

## Escalation Conditions

Escalation is required when:

- Customer references a specific state consumer protection law or statute that may override standard policy
- High-value orders (above $500) involve disputed return eligibility
- Customer has initiated a chargeback dispute and resolution through standard channels is time-sensitive
- Conflicting information exists between the order system and what the customer was told at checkout
- Customer claims a policy commitment was made verbally or through a chat interaction that contradicts current written policy

### Appendix A: Detailed Application Guidelines and Training Scenarios In order to ensure that all agents apply this policy correctly across diverse customer scenarios, the following detailed guidelines and case studies have been appended to this document. These scenarios are drawn from historical support logs and represent common edge cases where policy application requires nuanced judgment. It is imperative that all support staff review these scenarios regularly. The consistent application of policy is the foundation of our customer trust and regulatory compliance program. 1. **Scenario Assessment Methodology:** Before reviewing historical cases, agents must understand the core methodology for scenario assessment. First, immediately establish the factual timeline of the customer interaction. This means reviewing all system timestamps, starting from the exact moment of order placement, up to the precise second the carrier logged a delivery event. Do not rely on customer accounts of timeframes if they conflict with system logs. Second, review the customer’s interaction history. Are there past instances where similar policies were applied? If so, continuity in decision-making is preferred, unless the previous decision was explicitly marked as an error in the quality assurance (QA) audit. Third, consult the inventory management system to confirm the exact SKU and its assigned categorization at the time the order was placed. Categories can occasionally shift due to catalog updates; the category active at the time of purchase is the governing one. 2. **Cross-Departmental Coordination Protocol:** Often, policy resolution requires input from departments outside standard tier-1 support. For example, involving the logistics liaison team is necessary when carrier tracking states a package was delivered, but GPS coordinates suggest misdelivery to a neighboring postal code. In such cases, agents must open a sub-ticket tagged for the logistics team and place the main customer interaction in a pending state for no more than 48 hours. When coordinating with the finance department regarding complex refund routing (e.g., split payments involving store credit and an expired credit card), agents must provide the exact transaction ledger IDs. Do not attempt to manually calculate prorated refunds for multi-item orders with partial cancellations; always use the automated refund calculation tool. 3. **Regulatory Audit Readiness:** It is critical to recognize that every ticket resolved under this policy may be subject to external regulatory audit, particularly in jurisdictions with robust consumer protection authorities. Therefore, the case notes must be written in clear, professional language that explicitly cites the relevant section of this policy document. For example, rather than writing "Denied return because item is opened," agents must write "Return request denied pursuant to Section 3 of the Hygiene-Restricted Products policy, as photographic evidence confirmed the factory seal was broken." This level of precision protects the platform during compliance reviews. 4. **Handling Customer Escalation Threats:** Customers may occasionally threaten to involve third-party dispute resolution services, credit card chargebacks, or social media exposure when a policy is enforced against their favor. Agents must remain objective and de-escalate without compromising the integrity of the policy. The standard response should calmly reiterate the policy provision, explain the rationale behind it, and outline the formal internal appeals process if one applies. Agents are authorized to offer a one-time goodwill credit of up to $15 to de-escalate tense situations, provided the customer account is in good standing and the interaction does not involve suspected fraud. This goodwill credit must not be framed as an admission of fault or a reversal of the policy decision. 5. **System Downtime Contingencies:** In the event that the primary order management system experiences degraded performance or a full outage, agents must not issue definitive resolutions based on incomplete cached data. Instead, invoke the standard downtime communication protocol, informing the customer that their request has been logged and will be processed immediately upon system restoration. If a policy deadline (e.g., the end of a 30-day return window) expires during a system outage, the system will automatically grant a grace period equivalent to the duration of the outage plus 24 hours. Agents manually reviewing such cases must apply this grace period proactively. 6. **Continuous Improvement Feedback Loop:** The contents of this policy are subject to quarterly review by the Global Policy Committee. Agents who identify recurring customer friction points or ambiguities in the text are encouraged to submit annotated examples through the internal policy feedback portal.
